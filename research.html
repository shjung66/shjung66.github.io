Research

Research focuses on musculoskeletal rehabilitation, exercise therapy, wearable robotics, digital healthcare, AI-based prediction models, occupational health, and sports biomechanics.

Research Areas

Musculoskeletal Rehabilitation and Exercise Therapy

Clinical and biomechanical studies on musculoskeletal dysfunction, including low back pain, thoracic hyperkyphosis, shoulder dysfunction, lower-limb alignment, and exercise-based intervention.

Wearable Robotics and Robot-assisted Rehabilitation

Research on wearable hip exoskeletons and robot-assisted gait rehabilitation, with a focus on trunk and lower-limb muscle activity, gait training, and clinical application after surgery or in older adults.

Digital Healthcare and Wearable Sensor-based Assessment

Development and application of digital healthcare tools, smartphone-based measurement methods, wearable sensors, and clinically applicable assessment systems.

AI-based Prediction Models in Physical Therapy

Development of machine learning and deep learning models for musculoskeletal assessment, diagnosis prediction, and treatment outcome analysis.

Sports Biomechanics and Taekwondo Movement Analysis

Biomechanical analysis of Taekwondo movements using kinematic variables, muscle activity, physical characteristics, and video-based tracking methods.

Occupational and Industrial Musculoskeletal Health

Workplace-based research on musculoskeletal health promotion, risk assessment, and prevention programs for industrial workers.

Selected Research Themes

  • Wearable hip exoskeleton-assisted gait rehabilitation
  • AI-based prediction models for musculoskeletal disorders
  • Smartphone- and wearable sensor-based movement assessment
  • Taekwondo movement analysis and sports biomechanics
  • Occupational musculoskeletal health and prevention programs
  • Exercise-based intervention for low back pain, shoulder dysfunction, and postural alignment
  • Thoracic kyphosis, postural alignment, and sleep-related musculoskeletal outcomes
  • Rehabilitation exercise programs for older adults and clinical populations
